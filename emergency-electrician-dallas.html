Common Electrical Emergencies We Handle

Don't wait for electrical problems to get worse - Call us immediately if you experience any of these issues

⚡
CRITICAL

Complete Power Outage

Your home has lost all power while neighbors have electricity. Could be main panel failure, service line issue, or meter problems.

DANGERS:
  • ▸Food spoilage
  • ▸No HVAC
  • ▸Security system down
  • ▸Medical equipment failure
🔥
CRITICAL

Burning Smell / Smoke

Burning plastic smell from outlets, switches, or electrical panel. This indicates overheating wires or failing connections.

DANGERS:
  • ▸Electrical fire risk
  • ▸Wire insulation melting
  • ▸Carbon monoxide
  • ▸Structure damage
⚠️
URGENT

Sparking Outlets or Switches

Seeing sparks when plugging in appliances or flipping switches. Indicates loose connections or short circuits.

DANGERS:
  • ▸Fire hazard
  • ▸Equipment damage
  • ▸Electrical shocks
  • ▸Arc flash burns
💥
URGENT

Repeated Circuit Breaker Trips

Breakers constantly tripping even after resetting. Circuit overload, ground fault, or short circuit present.

DANGERS:
  • ▸Overheated wiring
  • ▸Fire risk
  • ▸Equipment damage
  • ▸Electrical system failure
⚡
CRITICAL

Electrical Shocks from Outlets

Getting shocked when touching outlets, switches, or appliances. Indicates dangerous wiring faults or grounding issues.

DANGERS:
  • ▸Serious injury
  • ▸Heart arrhythmia
  • ▸Burns
  • ▸Electrocution risk
💧
CRITICAL

Water in Electrical Panel

Water leaking into your electrical panel from roof, plumbing, or flooding. Extreme danger of electrocution.

DANGERS:
  • ▸Electrocution
  • ▸Panel failure
  • ▸Total power loss
  • ▸Fire hazard
🌩️
URGENT

Storm Damage

Lightning strike, tree on power lines, or storm-related electrical damage. Service line or meter damage.

DANGERS:
  • ▸Power surge damage
  • ▸Fire risk
  • ▸Exposed wires
  • ▸Electrocution
🔌
HIGH

Dead Outlets Throughout Home

Multiple outlets not working in different rooms. Could indicate main panel issue or major circuit failure.

DANGERS:
  • ▸Hidden wiring damage
  • ▸Progressive failure
  • ▸Fire risk
  • ▸Inconvenience
💡
HIGH

Flickering Lights Throughout House

All lights flickering, not just one room. Indicates loose service connection or utility company issue.

DANGERS:
  • ▸Electrical damage
  • ▸Appliance failure
  • ▸Fire hazard
  • ▸Power loss

What to Do During an Electrical Emergency

Follow these steps to stay safe while waiting for emergency electrician

1

Call Emergency Electrician FIRST

Call (682) 466-2130 immediately. The sooner you call, the sooner we can dispatch help. Do NOT attempt repairs yourself.

2

Turn Off Power at Main Panel (if safe)

If you can safely access your electrical panel, turn off the main breaker. Do NOT touch it if there's water, smoke, or visible damage.

3

Evacuate if Necessary

If you smell burning, see smoke, or suspect electrical fire - evacuate immediately and call 911. Your safety is more important than property.

4

Keep Away from Damaged Areas

Stay away from sparking outlets, exposed wires, wet electrical equipment, or damaged panels until electrician arrives.

5

Don't Use Water

Never use water on an electrical fire or near electrical equipment. Water conducts electricity and can cause electrocution.

6

Wait for Licensed Electrician

Even if power is restored, wait for our electrician to inspect and fix the issue properly. Electrical problems rarely fix themselves.
