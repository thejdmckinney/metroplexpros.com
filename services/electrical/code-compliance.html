Common Code Compliance Issues

🔌

GFCI & AFCI Protection

Required in bathrooms, kitchens, garages, outdoors, and bedrooms. Prevents shocks and fires.

✓GFCI outlets in wet areas
✓AFCI breakers in bedrooms
✓Kitchen counter outlets
✓Outdoor circuits
✓Garage outlets
✓Basement locations
⚠️

Overloaded Circuits

Too many devices on one circuit violates code and creates fire hazards.

✓Add new circuits
✓Redistribute loads
✓Upgrade panel capacity
✓Dedicated appliance circuits
✓Split kitchen circuits
✓Separate lighting circuits
🔧

Outdated Wiring

Knob-and-tube, aluminum, or ungrounded wiring must be updated for safety and code.

✓Replace old wiring
✓Add ground wires
✓Update aluminum connections
✓Rewire problem areas
✓Install proper boxes
✓Ground all outlets
⚡

Panel & Breaker Issues

Federal Pacific, Zinsco, or undersized panels don't meet modern code requirements.

✓Replace unsafe panels
✓Upgrade panel size
✓Add surge protection
✓Proper breaker sizing
✓Label all circuits
✓Arc-fault breakers

⚠️ Dangerous Code Violations

These violations require immediate attention for safety:

  • ⚠️Federal Pacific or Zinsco electrical panels (fire hazard)
  • ⚠️Aluminum wiring without proper connections
  • ⚠️Missing GFCI protection in wet areas
  • ⚠️Exposed or damaged wiring
  • ⚠️Overloaded circuits or improper wire sizing
  • ⚠️Ungrounded outlets in older homes
  • ⚠️DIY electrical work without permits
